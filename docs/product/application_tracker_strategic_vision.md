# Application Tracker Strategic Vision

## Purpose

Application Tracker exists to help a jobseeker achieve their next job goal with less friction, better judgement, and stronger follow-through.

It is not just a board for organising work. It is a personal job-seeking operating environment that helps a user:

- identify the right next opportunities
- decide what is worth pursuing
- complete real application work
- reuse and improve application artefacts over time
- move smoothly between internal workflow and external tools
- adapt quickly as the user’s goals change

## Product thesis

A job search has two equally important sides:

1. **Workflow orchestration**  
   The user needs a clean, action-oriented way to organise work, track progress, and feel momentum.

2. **Real application graft work**  
   The user needs practical help finding roles, assessing fit, shaping applications, managing artefacts, and navigating the fragmented ecosystem in which applications are actually completed.

Most tools handle one side poorly or ignore the other.

Application Tracker should do both.

## Product vision

**Application Tracker is the calm, intelligent workspace that helps a jobseeker move from intent to next role.**

It should combine:

- clear workflow management
- personally relevant AI guidance
- persistent artefact awareness
- low-friction transitions into the external systems where work actually happens

## The user’s goal

The system should be anchored on the user’s current job-seeking goal.

That goal may be:

- progressing a career
- switching careers
- increasing compensation
- finding stability quickly
- improving flexibility or work-life fit
- changing sector, location, or work mode
- any combination of the above

The goal may change over time and the system must respond quickly when it does.

## Strategic product principles

### 1. The product serves the user’s current job goal
The application should either infer or explicitly capture what the user is trying to achieve now.

This intent should shape:

- job recommendations
- AI assessment and guidance
- workflow priority
- artefact suggestions
- application support

### 2. Organisation and execution are equal citizens
The board is important, but it is only one part of the product.

The product must help the user not only organise work, but also complete meaningful work.

### 3. AI guidance must be embedded in the flow of work
AI should not sit in a separate novelty layer.

It should appear where the user needs it, for example:

- on incoming job recommendations
- on saved jobs
- in job workspaces
- when preparing applications
- when reusing artefacts
- when deciding next steps

### 4. The system should learn from the user’s corpus
Over time, the user builds a valuable corpus including:

- resumes
- cover letters
- tailored variants
- notes
- applications
- interview material
- captured jobs
- communications and outcomes

This corpus should improve the relevance and usefulness of guidance.

### 5. External workflow is part of the product
Real application work often happens outside the tracker.

The design must treat transitions to other tools as first-class product moments, including:

- employer and ATS application flows
- job boards such as LinkedIn
- document editors
- email
- future integrations where feasible

### 6. Entry points should respect user intent
Not every job enters the system at the same stage.

- **System-recommended jobs** should flow into an inbox or triage surface.
- **User-curated jobs** saved intentionally from elsewhere are already pre-qualified by the user and should enter a more appropriate stage.

## Core experience promise

Every time the user engages with the product, it should help them answer:

- What roles are most worth my time now?
- What work needs action today?
- What guidance can help me improve my chances?
- Which artefacts should I use or adapt?
- What is the cleanest next step, including outside this product?

## Strategic pillars

### Pillar 1: Goal-aware guidance
Capture and maintain the user’s current job-seeking intent and use it to shape recommendations and support.

### Pillar 2: Workflow momentum
Provide a clean, action-oriented workspace that reduces cognitive load and makes progress visible.

### Pillar 3: Application execution support
Help the user perform real work through assessment, drafting support, artefact reuse, and contextual guidance.

### Pillar 4: Artefact intelligence
Treat user artefacts and history as a durable asset that improves the system over time.

### Pillar 5: Seamless transitions
Make handoffs into external tools and back again low friction and coherent.

## Product model

Application Tracker should be understood as a connected set of product capabilities:

1. **Intent layer**  
   User goals, preferences, constraints, and changing priorities.

2. **Opportunity layer**  
   Recommended jobs, captured jobs, and curated jobs from external discovery.

3. **Workflow layer**  
   Inbox, active stages, closed stages, follow-up and momentum management.

4. **Work layer**  
   Application preparation, artefact selection, writing support, interview preparation, and communications tracking.

5. **Learning layer**  
   Outcomes, corpus growth, reusable patterns, and improved AI relevance.

## Product outcome

The product should help the user do three things better than they can today:

- spend time on the right opportunities
- complete stronger applications with less wasted effort
- maintain momentum across a fragmented job-seeking process

## Success criteria

At a product level, success means the user feels:

- clearer about what to do next
- better supported in doing it
- more confident that the system understands their goal
- less burdened by fragmentation across tools
- more effective over time as their corpus grows

## Strategic summary

Application Tracker should not be positioned as a job board companion or a kanban app with AI.

It should be positioned as a goal-aware job-seeking workspace that combines:

- opportunity intake
- intelligent guidance
- execution support
- artefact reuse
- workflow momentum
- seamless offboard transitions

That is the long-term product vision.
